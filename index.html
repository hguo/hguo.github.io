Hanqi Guo is a computer scientist and specializing in visualization, analysis, and machine learning for scientific data. He is an awardee of the DOE Early Career Research Program in 2022. He has received the best paper awards in IEEE VIS, IEEE PacificVis, and ChinaVis, premier conferences in the visualization research community. Please see my curriculum vitae for more details.

Funded Ph.D. Positions in Dr. Hanqi Guo's lab at the Ohio State University

Dr. Hanqi Guo is seeking several highly self-motivated Ph.D. students with a start date in Spring/Fall 2023 at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) at the Ohio State University (OSU). Successful candidates will work with Dr. Guo, an incoming Associate Professor at CSE, on multidisciplinary research involving visualization, analysis, and machine learning. Students will have opportunities to participate in summer research programs at Argonne National Laboratory and other government laboratories to work closely with domain scientists in climate, plasma physics, and material scientists. Please see our website for more details on our research: https://www.mcs.anl.gov/~hguo.

Qualifications:

  • A bachelor's degree or higher in computer science, mathematics, physics, or a related field
  • Strong motivation and interest in a research career
  • Good programming and communication skills
  • (Optional) prior research experiences

Candidates are encouraged to send the following documents to guo.2154 [at] osu [dot] edu with the subject starting with "[2023 PhD Application]"

  • Latest CV
  • Statement of research interests
  • Bachelor's (and Master's, if applicable) transcript
